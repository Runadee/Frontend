Biography

Emily Brontë was an English novelist and poet who is best known for her only novel, Wuthering Heights. Born on July 30, 1818, in Thornton, West Yorkshire, Emily was the fifth of six children. She spent most of her life in Haworth, a village in the Yorkshire moors.

Emily's early life was marked by tragedy. Her mother died when she was just three years old, and her two eldest sisters died within a year of each other. Despite these hardships, the Brontë siblings were close and found solace in their creative pursuits.

Emily Brontë's poetry is characterized by its intensity and emotional depth. Her novel, Wuthering Heights, published in 1847, is considered a classic of English literature and a masterpiece of Gothic fiction.

Works

  • Wuthering Heights (1847)
  • Poems by Currer, Ellis, and Acton Bell (1846)
